PCB Design — Practical Rules of Thumb & Lookup Guide

A practical, experience‑based checklist for designing low‑noise, reliable and manufacturable printed circuit boards.

Schematics

  • Group circuits by function
  • Use clear signal path (left → right)
  • Use standard symbols
  • Label nets clearly
  • Annotate and document
  • Mark polarity & pin‑1 clearly
  • Label connectors meaningfully
  • Include board name, revision, and date

Stack‑up & planes

  • Decide stack‑up early
  • Prioritize a solid ground plane first, then power plane, keeping the two planes directly above each other to minimize loop area
  • On 2‑layer boards, use copper pours for power and ground and stitch top/bottom pours with plenty of vias
  • Avoid split planes where possible
  • Prefer star / single‑point power distribution over daisy‑chaining, especially for noisy or sensitive loads
  • Never route high‑speed signals across plane splits
  • Prefer routing high‑speed signals adjacent to a ground plane
  • Use board zoning: separate analog, digital, and power sections — keep sensitive analog furthest from noisy digital and power circuitry

Signal integrity & routing

  • Signals always return under their trace in the nearest plane
  • Minimize trace lengths and loop area
  • Add ground stitching vias near layer transitions
  • Keep clocks short and isolated from sensitive nets
  • Use 45° angles instead of 90° (cleaner routing & RF‑friendly)
  • Above 80 MHz, avoid through‑hole components where possible to reduce parasitic inductances. Traces longer than 10 cm can be a problem for FM‑band noise
  • To reduce crosstalk, avoid long parallel runs; if unavoidable, keep ≥ 3× trace‑width spacing
  • Avoid routing noisy traces near board edges
  • No unrelated components should be closer than 2.5 cm to the crystal
  • Give unused inputs a defined logic state per the datasheet (pull‑up or pull‑down)
  • Where pertinent, use small source‑series resistors on MCU GPIO to slow edges and add RC low‑pass filtering only on slow, non‑critical signals
  • Differential pairs: keep equal length, constant spacing, matched impedance
  • Length‑match only when timing or protocol requires it

Decoupling & power integrity

  • Use decoupling capacitors
  • Place 0.1 µF MLCC close to each IC power pin
  • Keep the loop between cap–VCC–GND as short as possible
  • Add bulk capacitors per rail/region (1–10 µF typical)
  • Use planes or wide traces for power
  • Avoid narrow neck‑downs on power paths
  • Star‑route sensitive analog supplies where possible

Vias

  • Avoid vias on high‑speed nets where possible
  • Keep differential pair vias symmetrical
  • Use stitching vias around RF/analog areas
  • Avoid via‑in‑pad unless they are filled & plated (VIPPO)
  • Use thermal relief spokes on vias to planes for solderability

Voltage transients & ESD protection

  • Protect all external I/O at connectors against ESD / surge
  • Use TVS diodes close to connectors, with a short, wide path to ground (or chassis)
  • Choose low‑capacitance TVS for high‑speed data lines (USB, HDMI, etc.)
  • Where protocol allows, add series resistors or small RC filters on long or noisy lines to limit surge current and ringing
  • For power inputs, consider reverse‑polarity protection (diode or ideal FET), input TVS (and possibly a fuse or resettable fuse), and an LC/π filter to keep conducted noise out/in
  • Keep the ESD current return path local: don’t force ESD currents to run through sensitive circuit grounds
  • Use common‑mode chokes on long cables if emissions or susceptibility are a problem

Creepage & clearance (important for >30 V)

  • Follow IPC‑2221 or applicable safety standards
  • Increase spacing in humid/dirty environments
  • Slots can increase creepage where needed

High current & temperature

  • Know current & temperature ranges
  • Design traces using calculators
  • Use special widening / copper weight for high‑current paths — link

Manufacturability (DFMA)

  • Where possible, keep SMD components on one side
  • Component spacing appropriate for assembly
  • Accurate footprints (IPC‑7351 recommended)
  • Provide fiducial marks for pick‑and‑place
  • Arrange consistent orientation where possible
  • Consider panelization early if going to volume
  • Know the PCB fab limits (trace width, spacing, via size, mask expansion)

DFMA tools: https://jlcdfm.com/

Design Rule Check

  • Configure the ECAD DRC before layout begins — guide

Recommended baseline limits (check fab docs first):

  • Min trace width: ~6 mil typical
  • Clearance: ~6 mil typical
  • Via drill: ≥0.3 mm typical
  • Mask expansion: 3–5 mil

Design for Test (DFT)

  • Add test points to key nets (GND, 3V3, 5V, reset, debug, UART, etc.)
  • Ensure probe access space
